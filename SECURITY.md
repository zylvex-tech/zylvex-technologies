# Security Policy

## Reporting a Vulnerability

Zylvex Technologies takes security seriously. If you discover a vulnerability in any Zylvex product or service, we ask that you disclose it responsibly.

### How to Report

**Email:** [security@zylvex.com](mailto:security@zylvex.com)

Please include the following in your report:

- A description of the vulnerability and its potential impact
- Detailed steps to reproduce the issue
- Any proof-of-concept code or screenshots
- Your name and contact information (optional, for credit)

### What to Expect

| Step | Timeline |
|------|----------|
| Acknowledgement of your report | Within **48 hours** |
| Initial triage and severity assessment | Within **5 business days** |
| Status update with remediation plan | Within **10 business days** |
| Fix deployed (critical/high severity) | Within **30 days** |

### Guidelines

- **Do not** publicly disclose the vulnerability before we have addressed it.
- **Do not** access, modify, or delete data belonging to other users.
- **Do not** perform denial-of-service attacks or social engineering.
- Make a good-faith effort to avoid disrupting our services.

### Scope

The following are in scope for responsible disclosure:

- All backend services (Auth, Spatial Canvas, Mind Mapper, Social, Realtime, Notifications)
- Web application (`web-app/`)
- Mobile applications (Spatial Canvas, Mind Mapper)
- Infrastructure and deployment configurations

### Recognition

We appreciate the security research community. Reporters who follow this policy will:

- Receive credit in our security acknowledgements (if desired)
- Not face legal action for good-faith security research

### Out of Scope

- Vulnerabilities in third-party dependencies (report directly to the maintainer)
- Issues already known or previously reported
- Social engineering or phishing attacks against Zylvex employees
