# Product Vision

## Mission

> **Zylvex makes the physical world programmable for human knowledge and connection.**

We are building the foundational infrastructure layer for spatial-social computing — enabling people to attach persistent digital knowledge to physical locations and to map their cognitive focus as shareable, AI-enhanced knowledge trees.

---

## North Star

**Make space the interface for human knowledge.**

In 10 years, the dominant computing interface will not be a glass rectangle — it will be the world itself. Knowledge, collaboration, and social connection will happen *in physical space*, augmented by AI, accessible through AR glasses, spatial audio, and biometric sensors.

Zylvex is building the foundational API and social graph layer for this world — the way Twilio became foundational for communications, or AWS for cloud compute.

---

## The Two Products

### 🗺️ Spatial Canvas — The World as a Canvas

Spatial Canvas treats every physical location as a programmable surface. Users drop digital anchors — text, images, video, audio — at GPS coordinates, creating a persistent digital layer over reality.

**Core insight:** The most valuable content is often tied to a specific place. "The best ramen in Tokyo" isn't just a restaurant review — it's a piece of knowledge that only makes sense *at that location*. Spatial Canvas makes that knowledge discoverable by anyone who stands there.

**The AR social graph:** When enough people contribute to the same places, a spatial social network emerges. You can see what your followers have left behind in a café, a park, a museum. The physical world becomes socially annotated.

### 🧠 Mind Mapper — Cognition Made Visible

Mind Mapper is the first mind-mapping application that integrates **biometric cognitive data**. By connecting to BCI (brain-computer interface) devices, it color-codes knowledge nodes by focus intensity — making your thinking process visible and shareable.

**Core insight:** The *context* in which you created an idea matters as much as the idea itself. A note written with 90% focus during deep work is qualitatively different from a casual thought jotted while distracted. Mind Mapper captures that context.

**The focus graph:** As users accumulate BCI session data, a picture emerges of *when* and *how* their best thinking happens — the foundation for AI personalization of knowledge work.

## The Spatial-Social Computing Category

We are defining a new computing category:

| Era | Interface | Social Layer | Knowledge Layer |
|-----|-----------|--------------|----------------|
| Web 1.0 | Browser/URL | None | Static documents |
| Web 2.0 | Mobile apps | Social networks | User-generated content |
| **Zylvex era** | **Physical space + AR** | **Spatial social graph** | **Location-anchored + biometric knowledge** |

Three technology waves make this possible **now**:
1. **AR hardware maturation** — Apple Vision Pro, Meta Quest 3, AR glasses becoming consumer products
2. **BCI accessibility** — Neurosity Crown, OpenBCI Ganglion, Muse headband under $300
3. **LLM integration** — AI can synthesize spatial + cognitive data into personalized knowledge

---

## Why Now

- **AR hardware is ready:** Apple Vision Pro shipped. Meta Quest 3 is mainstream.
- **BCI is consumer-grade:** Neurosity Crown ($999), Muse 2 ($250), OpenBCI ($500)
- **The spatial social graph is vacant:** No platform owns the spatial layer of human connection
- **Remote work created the problem:** Distributed teams lost the spatial context of collaboration — the whiteboard, the hallway conversation, the "I had this idea standing right here" moment

---

## Target Users

### Phase 1 (Now): Early Adopters
- **Knowledge workers** who mind-map, brainstorm, and take visual notes
- **Urban explorers** and local guides who want to annotate physical spaces
- **Developers** who want to build on the spatial API

### Phase 2: Mainstream
- **Teams** using collaborative mind mapping (replacing Miro/Notion)
- **Local businesses** using anchors for contextual marketing
- **Educators** building spatial learning experiences

### Phase 3: Platform
- **Enterprises** managing spatial knowledge (facilities, field operations, retail)
- **AR content creators** monetizing spatial content
- **BCI researchers** using Mind Mapper as a cognitive research platform

---

## Values

1. **Privacy by default** — BCI data is the most personal data imaginable. We never sell it.
2. **Open by design** — Public API, webhooks, and SDK so developers can build on top of Zylvex.
3. **Spatial first** — Every product decision asks: "does this make the physical world more useful?"
4. **Cognitive respect** — We do not use focus data to manipulate users; only to enhance their own understanding of their cognition.
